Intro

My name is Jesus Tapia, first-generation student of the Latinx descent. I am currently completing my Master of Science in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Dallas. I previously obtained my Bachelor of Arts in Economics and Latin American Studies from Pomona College.

I excell in math, science, and technology. Retrospectively, I realize my high school lacked a strong STEM department, and imposter syndrome persuaded me to pursue a degree in history and economics. While studying Economic Statistics, I was introduced to STATA - a statistical analysis software – through which I began to tap into my passion for computer programming. Constrained by time and graduation requirements, I graduated without exploring computer science courses but with the intention of learning to design and program software at the best and most fitting opportunity.

Following graduation, I participated in the American Economic Association Summer Program (AEASP) at Michigan State University where I continued learning STATA and was introduced to R. Although fascinated to work with the software, I felt limited by its applicability and realized that I would never feel satisfied working solely with statistical software programs. I applied and earned acceptance to a master’s in Information Systems & Technology at Claremont Graduate University, but to my surprise, the program's professors encouraged me to strive for a direct path through a master’s program in computer science. After a myriad of uphill battles, I earned acceptance to the Master of Science in Computer Science at The University of Texas at Dallas.

In the span of two years, I have learned to use a variety of integrated development environments and become proficient in Java and C++ while acquiring working knowledge of Python, C, C#, JavaScript, CSS, and HMTL. I am currently enrolled in OS Concepts and Web Programming Languages courses. I contributed to the development of a basic Unix shell capable of running interactive and batch modes with the capacity to execute parallel commands. I am excited to learn and to integrate JavaScript frameworks (NodeJS, ReactJs) and databases (MongoDB) to our ongoing property management project and look forward to further developing these skills.

Projects

Basic Unix Shell in C capable of executing commands in interactive and batch modes. Built on XV6, the basic shell will allow users to specify a search path, execute commands in parallel, and redirect output.

Ongoing collaboration with two partners to design and develop an online property rental business using HMTL, CSS, JavaScript and eventually React, NodeJS, and MongoDB. Users will be able to sign up for renter accounts and host accounts to list, reserve, and rate properties. Users will search properties/destinations by and save to favorites to plan future reservations.

Designed and coded in Java a program that generated a word puzzle. Leveraged hash map and quadratic probing data structures to store a dictionary of words and find words in the puzzle. The program generates a word puzzle of any dimension, prompting the user for any positive integer combination of rows and columns with the option to choose an enhance solving algorithm.
